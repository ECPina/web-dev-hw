STEP ONE:

Gathering References!

When building an alt character, it's good to gather some references to draw from. You can make your own or find other sources. I chose a more "Emo" character, which was a popular fashion style and music scene when I was in highschool. So I would be looking for things like:

  • Pixie hairstyles
  • Tattoos
  • Piercings
  • Dark, torn clothes

I often browse Goolge, Tumblr, or Pintrest for some quick ideas. I also like to walk around and see whats in the world around me. The best refrences come from your own expierences.

STEP FOUR:

Give your character a back story!

Now that we've drawn a character and given him a name, what's he like? What's his backstory? When trying to create an intersting history of a character, I like to ask myself:

  1. What kind of story am I writing?
  2. What are the themes I'm wanting to explore in that story?
  3. What kind of character do I want to write?

There's many approaches you can take. For me, I often start with what kind of story do I want to write? This can be as simple as the genre, such as: horror, fantasy, sci, romance, etc, to something more indepth, like a horror romance about a werewolf detective that keeps finding love in all the wrong places.

I then move on to the themes I'm looking to explore; gender, sexuality, spiritualism, racism, and/or other soical issues or commentary.

Next, what kind of person am I looking to create? Think spefically personaltiy traits. Are they grumpy? Quiet? Funny? Smart? Afraid of everything? A fun away to get some good ideas is to look at astrological charts or blood type persoanlities.

For example, Let's take Maddox here. Let's say he's:

  • Stubborn
  • Quiet
  • Creative

What could make Maddox have these traits? It's possible he's stubborn because that's how is father raised him. Maybe he's quiet because he's shy. And maybe he was encouraged to be creative because his mother was an artist. Notice how we already have a little history happening? The most imporant thing is to have fun and to think outside of the box. Write from your expierences to help make your characters feel dynamic.
